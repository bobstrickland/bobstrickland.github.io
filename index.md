I began learning to program on an Apple 2+ back in 1982. I rode my bicycle halfway across the tiny town we lived in to the library to work on it.

At 19 I enlisted in the Marine Corps, learned COBOL, and did mainframe programming for the next few years.
After my enlistment, I returned to New Orleans and earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science.  
It took me a few years to complete as I was also:
- An electrician in the Louisiana Air National Guard
- A Natural programmer going y2k bug-fixes
- Working the parts counter at a local motorcycle shop
- Maintaining COBOL/CICS code in the wire department of a local bank

After completing my degree:
- I began working as a contractor at a Navy center (SPAWAR) for a few years
- Moved to Baton Rouge to work on the TOPS Scholarship program for the Louisiana Department of Education
- Moved to Virginia to work for ValueOptions on their Military OneSource contract
- Moved down to Georgia to raise my children close to my extended family while continuing to work for ValueOptions.

Lots of life happened in the meantime.  I almost finished my Masters degree but a medical emergency interrupted that, and I've not yet been able to restart it.

Since my work on the Military OneSource project ended earlier this year, I've gotten my AWS Certified Developer certification, have launched 2 of my own AWS-hosted projects (both dockerized monolithic applications using Redis/ Postgresql/ S3/ SES).  I've written a simple AI Agent tool as a learning exercise, and I'm contemplating a microservices project to begin soon.

And that's about it for now, something else good coming soon....
