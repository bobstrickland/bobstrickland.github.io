I'm a full-stack Java developer with 25+ years of experience building 
secure, large-scale systems for federal healthcare and financial services. 
Most recently I spent 14 years as lead developer at Carelon Federal 
Services, where I owned the backend for Military One Source — a federal 
platform serving over three million users seeking mental health and 
insurance claim services.

My background sits at an interesting intersection: deep legacy system 
knowledge (Java, Spring Boot, Oracle, DB2, even COBOL where it still 
matters) combined with active investment in where the industry is heading 
— AWS certified in 2026, hands-on with Docker, PostgreSQL, and serverless 
caching, and regularly using AI-assisted development tools in my own 
engineering work.

Security has been a consistent thread throughout my career. I've 
hand-coded cryptographic implementations from RFC specifications, built 
drivers to utilize FIPS 140-2 compliant encryption interfaces, and at the graduate research 
level, implemented a proof-of-concept Windows kernel mode driver that 
exploited Direct Memory Access to capture keystrokes via GPU memory — 
alongside a companion detection tool using the PCI bus to expose 
GPU-resident malware. I understand security at an implementation level, 
not just a configuration level.

Before my software career I served in the U.S. Marine Corps, where I 
built manpower and finance systems in COBOL/CICS and was awarded the 
Navy Achievement Medal for technical innovation. That background shapes 
how I think about ownership, accountability, and building things that 
have to work.

Outside of work I'm building a few things for myself: an Android app 
called Japa — a mantra-counting app with some unusual design decisions 
driven by real user needs — and a personal AWS project where I'm getting 
hands-on with the cloud-native patterns I want to bring into my 
professional work.

I'm based in Lawrenceville, GA (Atlanta metro) and currently open to 
new opportunities.
